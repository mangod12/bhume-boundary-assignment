BhuMe Walkthrough

0:00
Opening

Hi, I am walking through my BhuMe boundary correction submission.

The main thing I want to show is that I treated this as a trust problem, not just a polygon-moving problem.

0:15
Task

The task is to read official plot boundaries, compare them against satellite imagery and the rough boundary hint layer, and output predictions.

Each plot is either corrected or flagged.

0:35
Scoring

The scoring dimensions are accuracy, confidence calibration, and restraint.

That changed the approach from maximum corrections to trustworthy corrections.

0:55
Hidden set

The public examples are tiny, so I used them as a sanity check, not as training data.

I did not hard-code public-truth shifts.

1:15
Inputs

The inputs are input.geojson, imagery.tif, and boundaries.tif.

Imagery is primary. Boundaries are useful, but rough.

1:40
Raster alignment

The solver aligns boundaries.tif onto the imagery grid before using it.

Without that step, the hint layer can be misleading.

2:05
Method

For each plot, I rasterize the official boundary into a local patch and score nearby candidate shifts.

The candidate has to beat the official position.

2:35
Confidence

Confidence is based on evidence margin and ambiguity.

If the evidence is weak or noisy, the plot is flagged.

3:00
Restraint

A bad land-boundary correction is worse than no correction.

That is why many plots remain flagged.

3:25
Vadner result

Vadnerbhairav: 4 corrected, 2 flagged.

Median IoU is 0.888 versus official 0.612, and corrected public plots improve.

3:55
Malatavadi result

Malatavadi: 1 corrected, 2 flagged.

Median IoU is 0.763 versus official 0.510.

4:20
Ablation

The ablation shows boundary-only is not safe across both villages.

The final mode combines imagery and aligned boundary hints.

4:40
Failure modes

This can fail when imagery edges are weak, crop edges do not match legal boundaries, or hints are noisy.

In those cases, the solver flags.

5:00
AI use

I used AI to understand the assignment, audit the contract, debug GeoJSON, improve raster alignment, and verify with Playwright.

5:20
No runtime LLM

I did not use an LLM inside the solver.

The solver is deterministic and reproducible from the provided data.

5:50
Close

This is a reproducible correction pipeline that improves confident cases, flags uncertain cases, and treats confidence as part of the method.
